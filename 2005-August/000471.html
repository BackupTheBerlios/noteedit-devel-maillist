noteedit-devel-admin at berlios.de wrote:
>LilyPond team yesterday released a new development 2.7.5 version, which
>brought a new major feature IMO - text scripts (along with lyrics) now
>detect vertical&horizontal collision with other music elements (slurs,
>noteheads, other markings). Also, if the sillable in lyrics is to wide
>and doesn't fit, the space between notes is extended, to fit.
>
>I just thought I might tell you that as I usually had many problems with
>fine-tuning text markings in Lily document, but now it's all
>automatically. I don't need to use #padding anymore!:)
>
>
>Regards.
>- Matevž
>
>  
Hi Matevž,

thanks for this hint. I also ran in this often, so I thought to do a
oneline patch to add " " \raise #0.4 in every text item, so you can
adjust quickly the number in the .ly file, manually. \raise, #padding
and #extra-offset are all similar, and, not perfect. If lilypond would
do it automatically, it would be better. I experimented with 2.7.5, and
it works only when the notes that touch the text have a slur. Without
slur, there's still overlap. \raise and a slur would be compatible.

So, the lilypond developers are aware of the problem, sooner or later
they'll solve it :)

Btw: The paper selection in lilypond export is great! Landscape now
works well, I like it for proof reading. Thanks.

Regards,
Georg.
