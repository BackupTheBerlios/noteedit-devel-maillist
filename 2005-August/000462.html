Hi Matevž,

it's been long ago your question, and only now I find a relaxed moment
to think about it and offer my thought on it:

To overwrite and leave the not used time intact is a good feature.
Somehow it's an overwrite mode. And because I'm spoilt, because I have a
simple midi keyboard (and I know some piano playing) which I prefer to
input notes in NoteEdit, I think of two overwrite modes beeing attractive:

1) overwrite mode, which takes the notes and the timing from the new input
2) overwrite mode, which leaves the rhythmic structure unchanged, but
takes the notes/notes pitches from the new input

Often I find it hard work to specify the rhythm again and again. I would
in the new place put a copy with the rhythm I usualy did already, then
overwrite quickly with the midi keyboard, and will be done.
I'll try to be more on the wiki pages. Overwrite mode 1) will allow to
have an empty partiur with mostly only rests, and to fill it up with
music whereever you like.
