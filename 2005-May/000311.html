I think Wiki is a good place to introduce our development thoughts and 
ideas (although we must have in mind that this Wiki is public) about 
NoteEdit. Implementation of major new features and restructuring of 
NoteEdit should IMO look like this: introduce them on Wiki (and write as 
many info as possible, even some source code examples etc.), polish and 
crystal them up and then arrange them and make a compromise among other 
conflicting (if they are) ideas from other developers and only then, 
start implementing them. This way, we won't be doing double work, if 
someone's already on something like this and we'll all be aware of new 
things going on, how they work and what is (or should be:)) their 
purpose. I think anyone of us should be able to help an ordinary 
NoteEdit user if he has questions or troubles about any part of NoteEdit.

Well, other Wiki topics beside the development ones (ie. Wiki for users) 
are of course also welcomed:). That's just the first thought it came to 
me, how the Wiki could actually be useful at the moment.
