I searched the web for occurences of NoteEdit and found many sites 
pointing to the old Joerg's place. I went through all the first page of 
Google's hits and contacted their site administrators to correct the 
links to the new home. I also spotted TSE3 home being not up to date and 
contacted Pete. I think people shouldn't have the feeling that this 
project is dieing out by looking at the old NE's home. Sometimes I feel 
like people don't take this project seriously enough.

Second, I removed the QtCheckBox warning every time NoteEdit is ran, by 
deleting the value Checked with string "true" from mupWarning.ui. I'm 
not sure why this was good in the first place, because checked is 
already a binary value with bool true/false. If anyone knows, please 
correct it back.

Third, I removed the Lily warnings (like \property not found, WITHOUT 
slures, WITH semicolons etc.) from lilytest.cpp. I think they are not 
needed anymore now, because Lily export is tested enough and works fine 
and I also think they are obsolete (some ranging to <2.0 versions of 
lily) and shouldn't be mentioned anymore as convert-ly should convert 
the whatever-version-already-exported to the latest version of Lilypond 
syntax. The only warning I left was the Lilypond version found output 
(returns x.y.z or not found).

Fourth, I added my Ballade no.1 and Fugue no.1 I composed some time ago 
to NoteEdit examples. Anyone knows if the examples are installed to 
/usr/share/noteedit as well? Because currently, looking at /usr/local 
I'm not able to find them anywhere. They're only present in NoteEdit 
source dir.
